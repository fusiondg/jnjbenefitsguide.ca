New Pension & Savings Program — enrolment starts mid-December

Your enrolment checklist

Helping you save for the moments that matter

Moments that matter are different for each one of us. Are you focused on increasing your retirement income, or do you have shorter-term savings goals you want to achieve first?

This is your opportunity to choose pension and savings options that meet your needs and maximize your savings potential.

Important:

Enrolling in the new program is critical; your current selections will not roll over to the new program. Optional components are only available if you enrol during the enrolment period. If you don’t enrol, you will also miss out on Johnson & Johnson matching contributions and/or additional pension benefits earned.

Get ready! Here’s what you need to do.

Review the new Pension & Savings Program guide

The guide has key details about the new program and your options, decision making support and helpful scenarios.

Go to the Pension & Savings Program guide!

We held a series of information sessions in October. Did you miss out?
You can access a recording here. Go to Forms & Documents, then Pension Forms. Select Recorded Session — New Pension & Savings Program.

Review how much you have saved and think about the big picture

Go to the For Your Benefit (FYB) Portal to see how much you have saved to date. Take advantage of the tools to find out if your savings are on track.

Pension Projection Tool:
See how much DB pension you could earn at different retirement dates. You can run three retirement dates simultaneously to compare your retirement income at different ages. You can also see how a change in salary would impact your retirement income. Easily save or print your results to review with your spouse and/or a financial advisor.

Retirement Income Calculator (RIC):
Review your estimated retirement income from the plan, with the option to add in any personal savings you have in accounts like an RRSP or TFSA. You can set a retirement income goal and determine what your annual income in retirement will be.

Investor Questionnaire:
Answer questions related to your savings goals and tolerance with investment risk to determine the right investment options for you.

Go to the FYB Portal
Financial advisor
It can be beneficial to consult a personal financial advisor if you need additional support and/or guidance for your big picture retirement savings plan. If you don’t yet have a financial advisor, you can find one here.
Enrol starting mid-December

Log into the FYB Portal to use the enrolment tool and make your Pension & Savings Program selections.

Decide if you’re going to participate in the Optional DB

Watch this video to help you decide which option makes the most sense for you—Optional DB or Non-DB

Choose your Non-DB options and contributions (DC, RRSP, OAC, TFSA, Non-registered)

The Pension & Savings Program guide has some tools to help you with this decision.

Check out the considerations page.

Check out the scenarios to see what other employees are choosing.

Make investment choices for your Non-DB options

Your investment choices should be aligned with your risk tolerance, savings goals and time horizon. The Investment Questionnaire has got you covered!

Note: if you don’t make any investment choices for your Non-DB options, they will be invested in the default option, Phillips, Hager & North Balanced Pension Trust Fund. Please note this fund might not be right for you, and we strongly recommend that you make an investment choice that meets your needs.

Name your beneficiaries

Make sure your beneficiaries are up to date so that you can take care of your loved ones.

Submit your choices

Once you’re happy with your choices, don’t forget to submit them and complete any required forms (e.g., an updated beneficiary designation form). The enrolment tool will prompt you to print and complete forms, if necessary.
